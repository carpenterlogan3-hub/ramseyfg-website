The future of aerospace.

Yo-Yo Dyne is an aerospace company building novel airborne platforms that do what conventional aircraft and ground infrastructure can't.

Two flagship products anchor the company. AeroShade puts shade and weather protection in the sky — a tethered drone-swarm canopy that covers a crowd with zero ground footprint. Escape Pod lifts people clear of danger in minutes — a rapid stratospheric escape capsule for families, executives, and security details. Product 01 · Event Infrastructure

AeroShade — the sky's new canopy.

AeroShade's Nimbus Canopy is the first tethered drone-swarm shade system: a single seamless 25 m × 25 m fabric ceiling flown at roughly 100 feet by a silent 24-drone perimeter swarm.

It shades about 600 people with zero ground footprint inside the event area, deploys or retracts in 30 minutes, blocks 99% of UV and rain, and runs indefinitely on tethered power — replacing tents, poles, and rigging with a futuristic overhead experience for festivals, resorts, corporate activations, and disaster-relief operators. The global temporary-event-infrastructure market exceeds $12B and is growing 8% a year; each system reaches payback after roughly 12–13 deployments at gross margins near 80%.

Product 02 · Emergency Aerospace

Escape Pod — rise above any threat.

Escape Pod v1.0 is a rapid stratospheric escape system for families and VIPs: a pressurized, EMP-hardened, radar-stealth capsule that carries 6–8 people to a 50,000-foot sanctuary in under 30 minutes.

When wildfires, floods, storms, or a ground-level security event make staying put dangerous — and bunkers, life rafts, and aircraft are too slow or inaccessible — Escape Pod puts occupants 28–33 minutes above any ground threat. Built from carbon-fiber composite with onboard oxygen and stealth features including radar-absorbent materials and infrared suppression, it targets a $150B+ disaster-management market growing 15–20% a year across family, corporate, political, and military segments.
